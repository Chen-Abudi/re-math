# Fractions

## Concept

A fraction represents a part of a whole or, more generally, a ratio between two quantities.

It is written as:
a / b

Where:

- a = numerator (how many parts)
- b = denominator (total parts, cannot be zero)

---

## Why This Matters

Fractions are fundamental for:

- Ratios and proportions
- Probabilities
- Algebra and equations
- Real-world calculations (money, measurements, data)

---

## Subtopics

- Simplifying fractions
- Equivalent fractions
- Adding and subtracting fractions
- Multiplying and dividing fractions
- Fractions ↔ decimals ↔ percentages

---

## Key Ideas

- You need a **common denominator** to add/subtract
- Multiplication is straightforward (top x top, bottom x bottom)
- Division = multiply by the reciprocal
- FRactions are essentially **division in disguise**

---

## Intuition

Fractions are not just some pieces, they represent relationships.

**Example:**
1/2 doesn't just mean "half"

**It means:**

- 1 divided by 2
- A ratio of 1:2
- A point on the number line between 0 and 1

---

## Real-World Connections

- Money (e.g., half a price, discounts)
- Cooking (1/2 cup, 3/4 tablespoon, etc.)
- Data and probabilities
- Engineering ratios

---

## Developer Perspective

Fractions behave like operations:

- Division -> a / b
- Scaling -> multiplying fractions
- Normalization -> simplifying fractions

In programming:

- Floating point numbers ≈ fractions
- Precision issues often come from fraction representation

---

## Mental Model

**Think of fraction as:**

- Division
- Ratios
- Point on a number line

Not just some cake or pizza slices.

---

## Summary

Fractions are foundational concept that connect arithmetic, algebra, and real-world reasoning.
Mastering them will help build strong intuition for more advanced math and computational thinking.
